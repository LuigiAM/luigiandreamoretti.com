We have sensors tracking heart rate, sleep, and steps 24/7‚Äîbut mental health care barely uses this data. That gap felt solvable, and deeply personal. So I pivoted from clinical medicine to building the technology that could bridge it.

My PhD is letting me do both with rigor‚Äîco-designing wearable emotion monitoring with 87+ patients, carers, and clinicians to ensure it's clinically valid and genuinely useful.

I blend competencies most people keep separate: clinical insight, technical execution, and product thinking. I know why cEDA sensors matter for emotion detection. Current Focus

MEMoPAD Project

Multimodal Emotion Monitoring in Clinical Pathways for Anxiety Disorders

Building Wearable Emotion Monitoring for Anxiety Disorders

I'm developing a wearable emotion monitoring system using Google Pixel Watch 2 sensors (continuous electrodermal activity, heart rate, skin temperature) to help people with anxiety disorders understand their emotional patterns. The system captures real-world data and contextualizes it through mobile app interactions, providing insights for both patients and clinicians.

Co-designed with patients, carers, and NHS clinicians across 7 phases (5 completed), MEMoPAD ensures the technology is clinically valid, genuinely useful, and integrable into clinical pathways. This isn't just research‚Äîit's a functional prototype designed for real-world deployment.

The project bridges academic rigor with product thinking: published research validates the approach, while iterative co-design ensures people will actually use it. Currently recruiting for Phase V (implementation testing) and preparing thesis defense for Fall 2026.

Graduating Fall 2026. Exploring how to bring this technology to millions of users‚Äîwhether through continued research, industry partnerships, or building a company.
